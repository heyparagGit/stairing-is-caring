Rules — strong suggestions for having fun and staying safe

  • Only one team member can be in the stairwell at a time
  • Team members must alternate
  • Stairs count up AND down
    • Taking an elevator down is allowed
    • Entrance into the stairwell will be staggered by ~15 seconds
  • USE CAUTION!
    • Stick to the right except to pass
    • If you are coming down quickly, be RESPONSIBLE
  • We are counting on you to stop exactly when your time is up… no finishing up floors please.

How to calculate number of steps

Formula

((last full floor completed/number of full floors) x total number of stairs at site) + extra stairs climbed

Example

If you are climbing a 19 story building that has 420 stairs from the top to the bottom: When your time is up if you are 3 steps past the 9th floor….. ((9/19) x 420) + 3 = 202

Tips

  • Gathering pledges/guesses
    • People can make flat pledges to make a guess (I have received anywhere from $5-$100 in the past from an individual)
    • They can also pledge per stair…. Half a penny per stair… 10 cents a floor, whatever!
    • Some good motivators are that we are doing it for charity, they want to raise more than other floors/ groups, they want to win a lunch with you!
  • What you need to bring race day:
    • Stop watch that you can pass back and forth
    • Water to leave at the bottom of the stairwell
  • What you need to do after:
    • Notify your best guessers, take them to lunch
    • Track down outstanding pledges

Training

  • It is definitely a good idea to train for this event starting about 4-6 weeks before.
  • Some tips include:
    • Start slowly. Start with 1x a couple times a week and slowly increase your reps.
    • Be consistent. Once you start, just keep moving! Practice coming down.
    • For an extra challenge try it with a weighted backpack during training. You’ll feel light as a feather on race day!
